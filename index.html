The Bestiary

A natural history of the creatures this cohort has built and shipped.

Every specimen here is a paper-app: a small creature that is alive in the only way a webpage can be — it responds when touched. Each has been named by its collector, described in field notes, and shelved in the hall of the idea it best teaches.

This is a census, not a contest. Every creature the cohort ships belongs here, with its collector's consent. Nothing is ranked, required, or collected from visitors.

For collectors

How a creature gets in

Nothing to submit, and nothing new to write — you build in the open, and the museum comes to you. Every field below is something your paper-app already says or shows.

1 You build, in the open. Make a paper-app and publish it — a new repo, a GitHub Pages URL, any public link. That is the whole ask on your side.
2 Public is the trigger. The moment your work is public, it is fair game for the museum — there is no URL to submit and no form to send. You already know your course work lives in the open.
3 The curator finds it and names it. The curator checks for new work as it appears, then deals your creature a name at the Nomenclator and reads its field notes off what the app already does.
4 It hangs. The plate and placard go up from your published work, and your creature stands in its hall from that day on.
Yours to pull, always. Your course work is public, and you already know it can become a creature here — so there is no okay to give each time. Creatures carry first names or field-names only, and any creature is de-accessioned the moment its collector asks — no questions, no ceremony.
Wondering where creatures come from? The Instructor Study demonstrates each week's idea, Trailheads marks the paths into your own territory, and the Anthology keeps the world's specimens. What comes back from the trails gets housed here.
